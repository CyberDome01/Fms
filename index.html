What Is the MicrOrbit Financial Management System?

A quick overview of the tool and what it does

The MicrOrbit Financial Management System is a browser-based tool that helps you track, manage, and monitor all Go-To-Market expenses for Fiscal Year 2026–2027. It runs entirely in your browser — no login, no app to install. All data is shared live across your whole team via a secure cloud database.

💾
Auto-Saves to Cloud
Every change saves instantly to your shared team database. All team members see updates in real time.
⚡
Instant Updates
Every time you add an expense, budgets update instantly. No need to refresh or recalculate anything.
☁️
Shared Live Data
Data is stored in a shared cloud database. Every team member sees the exact same live data instantly.
📱
Works on Mobile Too
The dashboard is fully responsive — you can use it on your phone or tablet, not just your desktop.
📊

The Dashboard

Your home screen — a real-time snapshot of everything

The Dashboard is the first page you see when you open the tracker. It gives you an instant at-a-glance summary of your entire FY budget health.

1
4 KPI Cards at the top

Shows Total Budget, Total Spent, Remaining Balance, and number of expenses logged. These update automatically every time you add or delete an expense.

2
Spending by Category Donut Chart

A visual breakdown of how much has been spent in each category. The chart only appears once you have logged at least one expense. Hover over slices to see category names.

3
Recent Expenses Panel

Shows your 6 most recent expenses with date, category, vendor, and amount. Click "See all →" to go to the full expenses table.

4
Over-Budget Alert Banner

If any category has exceeded its budget, a red warning banner appears at the top of the Dashboard automatically naming which categories are over.

➕

Adding a New Expense

Log a new expense in under 30 seconds

You can add an expense two ways: click "Add Expense" in the left sidebar, or click the blue "＋ Add Expense" button in the top-right corner from any page.

1
Select the Date *

Pick the date the expense occurred. Today's date is pre-filled automatically — just change it if needed.

2
Choose a Category *

Select from the dropdown: Travel, Supplies, Legal, Subscriptions, Operating Expenses, or Training. New categories can be added by an admin.

3
Enter the Amount *

Type the dollar amount. As you type, the Budget Impact Preview panel below will show how much will remain in that category after this expense.

4
Add Vendor & Description *

Description is required. Vendor is optional but recommended — e.g. "Salesforce", "Delta Airlines", "Marriott". This helps when filtering and reviewing later.

5
Add Notes (optional)

Use this field for extra context like a PO number, project code, or approval reference.

6
Click "Save Expense"

The expense is saved instantly. The budget cards update immediately. You'll be taken to the Expenses table to confirm it was added.

💡
Budget Impact Preview — Once you select a category and type an amount, a live preview box appears showing: current budget, already spent, this new expense, and what will remain. This helps you spot overspending before you save.
✏️

Editing & Deleting Expenses

Fix mistakes or remove entries you no longer need

Go to All Expenses in the sidebar. Every expense row has two action buttons on the right side.

ActionButtonWhat It Does
Edit ✏️ Opens a popup where you can change the date, category, amount, vendor, description, or notes. Click "Save Changes" when done.
Delete 🗑 Permanently removes the expense after a confirmation prompt. The budget is updated immediately.
⚠️
Deletions cannot be undone. Once you confirm a delete, the expense is permanently removed. Double-check before confirming.

Understanding Budget Cards

How to read the budget overview page

Go to Budget Overview in the sidebar to see all categories. Each card shows:

🟢
On Track
Less than 75% of the budget has been spent. You're in good shape.
🟡
Near Limit
Between 75–99% of budget used. Time to slow down or request more budget.
🔴
Over Budget!
Spending has exceeded the allocated budget. An alert also appears on the Dashboard.
⚪
No Budget Set
The category has no dollar limit assigned yet. An admin can set it in Admin / Settings.

Each card also has a progress bar that fills green → orange → red as spending increases. Click "View Expenses" on any card to see only that category's expenses.

Exporting to CSV

Download your expense data for Excel or reporting

1
Go to All Expenses

Click "All Expenses" in the left sidebar.

2
Filter if needed

Optionally filter by category or search for specific expenses before exporting.

3
Click "⬇ CSV" button

Top right of the expenses panel. A file named _Expenses_FY2627_[date].csv will download automatically.

4
Open in Excel

The CSV includes: Date, Category, Description, Vendor, Notes, and Amount — ready for Excel pivot tables or reporting.

⚙️

Admin / Settings

Managing categories and budgets — for admins only

The Admin / Settings page is for whoever manages the budget. Regular users generally don't need to go here.

1
Add a New Category

Enter a category name, an annual budget amount, and an emoji icon. Click "Add Category". It will immediately appear in all dropdowns and budget cards.

2
Edit an Existing Category

Click "Edit" next to any category to change its name, budget amount, or icon. Useful when budgets are revised mid-year.

3
Delete a Category

Click "Del" to remove a category. If it has expenses linked, you'll be warned first. The expenses remain but become "unlinked".

4
Reset All Data ⚠️

The red "Clear All Data & Reset" button in the Danger Zone wipes everything and restores defaults. Only use this if you want to start completely fresh. Requires double confirmation.

Pro Tips

Get the most out of the MicrOrbit Financial Management System

🔖
Bookmark your SharePoint link — Pin the MicrOrbit link to your browser bookmarks bar so you can open it in one click every day.
🏷️
Always fill in the Vendor field — Even though it's optional, entering vendor names makes filtering and auditing much easier later (e.g. search "Salesforce" to see all subscription costs).
📅
Log expenses on the day they happen — It takes less than 30 seconds and prevents a backlog of receipts piling up.
📝
Use the Notes field for PO numbers — If your company uses purchase order numbers or project codes, put them in Notes so you can reconcile with finance reports.
⬇
Export to CSV monthly — Download a monthly CSV and save it to SharePoint as a backup record, or paste into your finance team's spreadsheet.
🔍
Use search + filter together — On the Expenses page, combine the category filter AND the search box to drill into specific expenses instantly (e.g. filter by "Travel" and search "Delta").
⚠️
Don't clear browser cache without exporting first — Data is stored in your browser's local storage. If you clear your browser data/cache, your expenses will be erased. Export a CSV backup first.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from the team

Will my data disappear if I close the browser?
No. Data is automatically saved in your browser's local storage every time you make a change. It will be there when you reopen the page — even days later.
Can my colleague see my expenses?
Yes! The tracker uses a shared cloud database (JSONBin). Everyone on the team who opens the same SharePoint link sees the exact same live data. Any expense you add, edit, or delete is instantly visible to all your colleagues.
What happens if I go over budget in a category?
The tracker will still let you log the expense — it won't block you. The category card will turn red and show "Over Budget!" and a warning banner will appear on your Dashboard.
How do I change a category's budget mid-year?
Go to Admin / Settings → click "Edit" next to the category → update the budget amount → Save. The change takes effect immediately across all cards and charts.
I accidentally deleted an expense. Can I recover it?
Unfortunately no — deletions are permanent and cannot be undone. Always double-check before confirming a deletion. This is why we recommend exporting to CSV regularly as a backup.
Can I add a category with no budget limit?
Yes. When creating a category in Admin, simply leave the budget as $0. The card will show "No Budget Set" in gray and tracking expenses will still work normally.
What does the % used on the progress bar mean?
It shows what percentage of that category's annual budget has been spent so far. Green = under 75%, Orange = 75–99%, Red = 100%+.
Why does the donut chart not show anything?
The donut chart only populates once you have logged at least one expense. Once you add your first expense, it will update automatically.
